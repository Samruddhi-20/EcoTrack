Data Analysis

1. By collecting data from waste bins across different areas, the system generates insights into the volume of waste produced in specific regions.

2. The system tracks how well waste segregation practices are being followed across various regions.

3. Data on vehicle routes, fuel consumption, and collection times is analyzed to assess the overall efficiency of the waste management system.

4. By analyzing the data collected, the system can help track progress towards sustainability goals, such as increasing recycling rates or reducing landfill waste.

Waste has significant negative impacts on the Earth, affecting ecosystems, public health, and the climate. When waste is improperly managed, it can lead to pollution of land, air, and water. Plastics, for example, take hundreds of years to decompose and often end up in oceans, harming marine life and entering the food chain. Hazardous chemicals from electronic and industrial waste can seep into the soil and groundwater, contaminating natural resources and affecting human and animal health.
